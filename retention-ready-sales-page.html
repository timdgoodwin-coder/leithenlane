The problem

Most early-stage brands have the same issue.

They're spending on ads, testing creatives, and chasing lower CPCs while customers they already paid to acquire quietly disappear after one purchase.

It's not a traffic problem. It's a retention problem. And until you fix the foundation, scaling your ad spend just means losing money faster.

  • Your post-purchase experience is a generic receipt email and then silence
  • You have an abandoned cart problem but no recovery flow running
  • Your email list is going cold because campaigns only go out when there's a sale
  • You're discounting to drive repeat purchases without knowing if the margin holds
  • You're not tracking the numbers that tell you whether retention is actually improving
"A 5% improvement in retention can increase profit by 25 to 95%. You don't need more customers. You need to keep the ones you have."

The email modules are written with Klaviyo in mind, since it's the platform most Shopify brands use for serious CRM work. The strategic frameworks apply to any email tool, but the specific flow logic assumes Klaviyo.

How long will it take to go through?

Plan for around 90 minutes per module if you're reading and taking action as you go. The goal is to implement, not just read, so build that time in. You can also work through individual modules if you already know where your biggest gap is.

I'm very early-stage. Is this relevant?

If you have customers coming through the door and you're spending anything on acquisition, yes. The earlier you build these foundations, the less revenue you lose in the meantime. The course is written for brands that are live and making sales, not pre-launch.
