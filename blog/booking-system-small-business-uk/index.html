Best Booking Systems for Small Businesses in the UK (Practical Comparison)

Samantha Goodwin
Founder, Leithen Lane

If you're managing bookings through a combination of texts, phone calls, a paper diary, and memory, you already know it's a mess. Double bookings, missed appointments, back-and-forth messages trying to find a time that works. It eats up time you could be spending on actual work.

The good news is that booking systems for small businesses have become really good and really affordable. Some are even free. Here's a straightforward comparison of what's out there.

Why bother with an online booking system?

Before I get into the tools, here's why this matters:

  • Customers can book when you're busy. If someone wants to book a haircut at 10pm on a Sunday, they can. Right now that's a lost booking because you're not going to answer your phone.
  • Automatic reminders reduce no-shows. Most booking systems send email and text reminders before appointments. For service businesses, no-shows can cost you 10-20% of revenue. Reminders cut that significantly.
  • You stop playing phone tag. No more "can you do Thursday?" "No, how about Friday?" "What time?" The customer sees your available slots and picks one. Done.
  • It looks professional. A "Book Now" button on your website or Google listing converts browsing customers into booked ones.

The best options for UK small businesses

I've set these up for businesses across Worcestershire, so this isn't theory. Here's what works:

  • Calendly (Free tier available). Simple, clean, works well. Great for consultants, coaches, and anyone who takes one-on-one appointments. The free version handles one appointment type. Paid plans start at about £8/month and add custom branding, group events, and payment collection.
  • Square Appointments (Free for individuals). If you already use Square for payments, this is a no-brainer. Free for solo operators. Handles bookings, automatic reminders, and you can take deposits or full payment at booking. Particularly good for hair and beauty, wellness, and personal services.
  • Setmore (Free tier available). A solid all-rounder with a generous free plan (up to 4 staff). Has its own booking page you can share or embed on your website. Good for small teams.
  • SimplyBook.me (Free tier available). More feature-rich than most. Free for up to 50 bookings a month. Paid plans are reasonable and unlock things like intake forms, packages, and gift cards. Good for health and wellness businesses.
  • Fresha (Free for beauty and wellness). Specifically built for salons, barbers, spas, and wellness businesses. Completely free for booking and calendar management. They make their money from payment processing, which is optional.

How to choose the right one

Ask yourself these questions:

  1. How many people need to use it? If it's just you, most free tiers will work. If you have a team, check the limits.
  2. Do you need to take payments at booking? If deposits or pre-payment would reduce your no-shows, pick a system that handles payments (Square, Calendly paid, SimplyBook).
  3. What industry are you in? Fresha for beauty, Calendly for professional services, Square for general retail and services. Pick something designed for your world.
  4. Do you want it on your website? All of these can be embedded on a website or shared as a link. But some embed more neatly than others. If your website is important to you, test the embed before committing.

Setting it up properly

The tool is only half the job. Setting it up well is what makes the difference:

  • Set realistic appointment durations. Include buffer time between appointments for cleanup, travel, or just catching your breath.
  • Block out lunch breaks and personal time. The system will fill every gap if you let it.
  • Customise your confirmation and reminder messages. Make them sound like you, not like a robot.
  • Add your booking link everywhere: website, Google Business Profile, Instagram bio, email signature, business cards.
  • Test it yourself. Book a fake appointment and see what the customer experience is like.
